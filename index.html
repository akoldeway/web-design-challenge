LatitudeVs. Project Summary

The equator is the imaginary line at the widest part of Earth which splits it into the northern and southern hemispheres. Regions long the equator are known to experience hot climates with little seasonal variation as they are furthest from the poles. The LatitudeVs. project was to analyze how weather changes the closer one is to the equator.

To perform this analysis, we pulled a random sampling of over 500 cities using the OpenWeatherMap API. With our dataset defined, we compared latitude of the cities versus temperature, cloudiness, wind speed, and humidity. Matplotlib was used to plot the various factors in comparison to the latitude of each location.

This site provides the output of that analysis in both visualiations and explanations of any trends or correlations found in the data.
